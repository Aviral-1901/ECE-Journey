# Day 1 : Transistors, Binary, Bitwise Operations

## What I learned
Silicon is used for making transistors. Silicon atoms form crystal lattice by combining with other 4 silicon atoms but there are no free electrons for conduction. So we add p-type or n-type impurity to make it conducting.                                                                              

Mosfet have source(n-type) on one side and drain(n-type) on other side and a channel(p-type) in between them. Gate is connected to channel via an insulating layer so when positive voltage is applied the electrons get attracted towards gate and electrons move from source to drain through channel.                                                                                     

0s and 1s are the voltage levels on a wire. 1 represents high voltage and 0 represents ground.             

Transistors switch in picoseconds but clock speeds are in nano seconds because the clock period should be greater than the time needed for signal to travel through critical path. Critical path is the longest path from transimission of signal from one register to another.

## Key Insight 
We can't keep any clock speeds as we want and we cant just increase clock speeds to increase speed the clock speed is limited by the worst case propagation(critical path) between registers so clock period must be greater than this delay. Bitwise operations allows to operate on individaul bits and they relate directly to trasistor level operations. Right shifting (>>) by n times divides the number in decimal by 2^n and left shifting(<<) multiplies the number in decimal by 2^n.
